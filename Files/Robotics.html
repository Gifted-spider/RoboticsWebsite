This week I finished my Rube Goldburg machine! The challenge was to start with a baseball and end with popping a balloon. We had to use 2-3 simple machines. The baseball goes down an incline place into a cardboard car. Then, the car would slam into a small box, which would in turn hit a bigger box with a string attached. This box was in a tunnel of cardboard with a rectangular hole on the top. Once the box was hit, The pulley would be triggered, making another baseball drop onto a wedge. The needle was attached to the wedge and slammed on top of the balloon, popping it. One of the biggest problems I had to solve was how to get the box with the string to not fly off because of the weight of the baseball on the pulley. I eventually used a piece of cardboard that was a little too big for the cut to stop the box. It could then be pushed our when the box was hit. I also had many problems with taping the carboard before it was all the way cut.

November 19, 2021

This week we started on our final project. We are working in groups and have to make a stable bridge out of spaghetti and wood glue. Our design is to have broken spagetti (at 2.5 inches) and alternate the glue parts with the middle of noodle. We had run some tests on the noodles obseving where the noddles were the strongest and what length of noodle was the strongest. We found that the glue actually made the noodles less strong, which was the opposite of our original hypothesis. We also found that shorter noodles were stronger, as there was less space to bend. In order to counteract the bendy glue problem. I had the idea to alternate the glue with pure noodle. We also thought to have a rail with shorter noodles to support the entire bridge.
